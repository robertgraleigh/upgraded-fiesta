How We Work

How We Begin Projects

What have you tried? What hasn't worked for you in the past? What has.

How We Track Progress

We use a variety of best-practice agile software tools, including Asana, Basecamp, Jira, and Trello. We manage project assets using tools Google Apps, Dropbox. Our goal is to create an easy accessible, Allow us to monitor the progress at any time, without creating signifant document project debt due to over-documentation. Depending on the size of your project. These tools allow us to accurately measure, and forecast the time remaining on your project milestones, and the epic features are part of your project.

How We Measure Success

Of course project success begins with delivering your project on budget and on time. But looking deeper, your project should align with your business objectives. A successful enables you to meet your businesss objectives, while poisting your web presence for your future goals and objectives down the road.

How We Communicate

Maintaining effective, transparent, and honest, communication between the project manager, product owner, and technical lead is essential to any successful product launch. Our direct communication be managed using Google Calendar, Chat, Meet, Slack. Telephone, or Email. Personally, I believe Slack + Video is more effective and than telephone email. But of course, we're open to using traditional tools if you prefer.

How We Handle Development

Your project's technical development work is completed in pre-defined, agile sprints. Each sprint is followed by a face-to-face meeting, to demo your completed work. Planned and completed work is tracked openly using industry-standard tools such as Asana, Basecamp, Confluence, and Jira. Visual product prototypes used in planning are built with Adobe XD, Invision, or Sketch. We'll store your code, documents, spreadsheets, and project assets using reliable tools like Dropbox, Github, and Google Drive.

How We Identify Objectives

Your website is the front door to your business. But your online presence also fits within the larger context of your business. We'll work together to assess your key business objectives, and examine how those will be tied closely and supported by your website or e-commerce store. to more clearly identify your project's measures of success.

  • Robert's Values

    Agility

    I believe that agility is central to the success and longevity of any modern technology professional. Being agile, and cultivating an ability to face the uncertain, quickly make decisions, and rapidly de-escalate situations, is essential in today's internet-based world.

    Community

    I believe in helping others through direct action in my own community. I do this in many ways, but I'm happiest when the answer to "What can I contribute today?" is action-driven, and I'm creating lasting value, rather than just "giving".

    Creativity

    I believe that creativity is innate in all people, but our ability to leverage creative thinking is often prevented by fear. It's not always the level of creativity, or novelty of a new idea or solution that matters most. It's having the courage to implement it.

    Empathy

    I believe that empathy is a buzz word for many of us. Starting out with empathy—for customers—for coworkers—for strangers can be difficult. Yet it's essential, and can be so powerful when it's genuine. A human connection can change everything.

    Problem-Solving

    I believe that effective problem-solving comes from an iterative approach. My intellectual curiosity and drive to identify and solve problems has led me to lean into many unique business obstacles and challenges throughout my career.

    Wisdom

    I believe we owe it to ourselves and those around us to "pay our successes forward". I actively seek out diverse leaders, from different geneartions, sharing accrued wisdom and experience freely with others, as part of my daily professional approach.
